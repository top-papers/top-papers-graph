\begin{abstract}
We present a reproducible agentic pipeline that performs literature-grounded hypothesis generation and self-critique for a narrow electrochemical energy question: designing Li-ion fast-charging profiles that reduce lithium plating risk. The system retrieves and structures evidence into a lightweight knowledge graph (GraphRAG), runs a multi-agent debate under an expert-derived checklist, and proposes charging protocols that are evaluated via open-source battery simulations (PyBaMM).
\end{abstract}
